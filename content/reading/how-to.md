I am a big fan of [xkcd](https://xkcd.com).<br> I also have it connected in my
daily digest, that I read, without fail, every morning.

When I heard that the author of this **iconic** comic strip had also published a
couple of books on scientific matters, I had to read them.

The book is structured as a manual on "how to" do certain things, mundane and
not - for every chapter there are some underlying

And for the first half of the book, I was mildly disappointed, the approach to
the questions, the resolution and everything in between was just absurd. I knew
that the comic strip was going for that kind of humor, but when it's condensed
in a couple of panels it feels brilliant - but when it occupies pages and pages,
at times, it just feels goofy and dorky.

However, I just needed to flex those humor muscles, by the half of the book,
everything looked shiny and brilliant again. So if you have the patience to give
it a try, you won't regret it.
