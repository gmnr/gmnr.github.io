About me

I have no CompSci background, but I am rather passionate about computers.

With no formal education and very little practical knowledge in the field (even though I ended up working for an IT company albeit for a less technical role that I had hoped for) I won't be hired for my passion.

Therefore, I have carved up a place for this hobby and so, in my spare time, I read a lot of resources, follow tutorials and try (and fail) the advent of code challenges.

Right now I have my eyes on the following topics:

  • System's Design
  • Software Optimization
  • Networking

Programming Languages

The first language I ever came in contact with is Python, and I have been in love ever since. Now I feel pretty confident with it (I am by no means a master) but I recognize the need to broaden my horizons. In the near future, I would like to try my hand at C or Java or Haskell.

Other languages that I use more often are

Why this blog?

I actually stated this blog as an exercise; I do a lot of wierd stuff in my spare time (programming wise) that ranges from little snippets of code just to prove a point with friends to more organic and coherent projects.

Furthermore, I have always been at a loss of word (for lack of a better term) and I believe that my communications skills are below average...

So this little corner of the internet achieves two things:

  1. allows me to share with the world the things that I am passionate about
  2. helps me building confidence in writing (I am always open for feedback, as gnarly as it could be)
